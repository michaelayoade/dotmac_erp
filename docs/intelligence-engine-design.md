## 1. Overview

### Philosophy

The Intelligence Engine is **not a chatbot**. It is a data-driven coaching system that:

1. **Reviews real organizational data** across all ERP modules
2. **Identifies patterns, risks, and opportunities** that humans would miss
3. **Pushes actionable coaching insights** to the right person at the right time
4. **Helps the company become better** through continuous feedback loops

### Three Operating Modes

| Mode | Trigger | Speed | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Proactive Insights** | Celery scheduled tasks analyze data, push findings | Background (minutes) | "Your cash conversion cycle worsened by 8 days this month" |
| **Contextual Coaching** | User views a page, relevant insights surface | Pre-computed (<100ms) | Manager opens team view, sees workload balance warning |
| **Interactive Analysis** | User asks a question in natural language | Real-time (2-5s) | "Why did expenses spike in February?" |

### Three Coaching Levels

```
                    +---------------------------+
                    |   ORGANIZATIONAL LEVEL    |
                    |   "How is the company     |
                    |    performing overall?"    |
                    +-------------+-------------+
                                  |
                    +-------------v-------------+
                    |      TEAM LEVEL           |
                    |   "How is my team doing   |
                    |    and how can I lead      |
                    |    them better?"           |
                    +-------------+-------------+
                                  |
                    +-------------v-------------+
                    |    INDIVIDUAL LEVEL       |
                    |   "How am I doing and     |
                    |    what should I work on?" |
                    +---------------------------+
```

### Key Design Principles

1. **Read-only on existing data** — The coach never modifies records. It only reads and generates insights.
2. **Pre-compute, don't real-time** — Most insights are generated by Celery tasks on a schedule, not on page load. Controls LLM costs and keeps pages fast.
3. **Structured output only** — Every LLM call requests JSON output. No free-form text to parse.
4. **Evidence-based** — Every insight includes supporting data. Users can verify. No black-box recommendations.
5. **Actionable** — Every insight has a concrete coaching action. Not "performance could be better" but "Schedule a 1-on-1 with Bob this week to discuss his KPI drop."
6. **Feedback loop** — Users mark insights as helpful/not relevant/inaccurate. This tunes prompt quality.
7. **Privacy-aware** — LLM sees aggregated metrics and anonymized codes, not raw names/emails.

## 3. Intelligence Domains

### 3.1 Cash Flow Intelligence

**Data Sources**: AR Aging, AP Aging, Banking, Sales Orders, Expense Claims, Payroll, Purchase Orders

| Analysis | Data Used | Insight Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Cash Conversion Cycle** | DSO (AR aging) - DPO (AP aging) + DIO (inventory turnover) | "Your CCC is 47 days (was 39 last quarter). DSO increased to 52 days because 3 customers are paying 15 days slower. DPO stayed at 30 days — you're paying suppliers faster than collecting." |
| **30-Day Cash Forecast** | Open AR by due date + Open AP by due date + Payroll schedule + Recurring expenses | "Next 30 days: Inflows N12.4M (AR due), Outflows N9.8M (AP N5.2M + Payroll N3.1M + Recurring N1.5M). Net: +N2.6M. But if Customer X pays late (15 days late last 3 months), you'll be at +N0.1M." |
| **Working Capital Trend** | Monthly Current Assets - Current Liabilities from GL | "Working capital declined 12% over 3 months. Root cause: inventory buildup (+N2.1M) while receivables stayed flat. Buying faster than selling." |
| **Payment Pattern Analysis** | Customer payment history vs. invoice due dates | "Customer ABC: avg 42 days (terms: 30). Customer XYZ: avg 18 days. Offer XYZ early payment discount. Put ABC on proactive collection." |
| **Expense Burn Rate** | Monthly expense claims + AP invoices + Payroll | "OpEx grew 8% MoM. Largest driver: Travel +34% (N2.1M to N2.8M). 3 people with international trips. Non-recurring? Verify." |

**Coaching Actions**:
- "Collect from your top 5 overdue accounts this week — that's N4.2M sitting idle"
- "Renegotiate payment terms with Supplier Y from Net-15 to Net-30 — frees N800K/month"
- "Payroll is N3.1M due on the 25th. Bank balance is N3.4M. Collect N1M before the 25th or draw on credit line"

### 3.2 Operational Efficiency

**Data Sources**: Workflow Executions, Approval Timelines, Task Completion, Expense Claims, Reconciliation, Period Close, Procurement Cycle

| Analysis | Data Used | Insight Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Approval Bottlenecks** | `submitted_at` to `approved_at` across all entities | "Average expense approval: 4.2 days. Manager Smith: 9.1 days (23 pending). Manager Jones: 1.3 days. Smith is your bottleneck — 67% of all pending approvals." |
| **Period Close Efficiency** | Fiscal period close timestamps, reopen counts | "Month-end close averaged 12 days (benchmark: 5-7). Bottleneck: AR reconciliation takes 4 days (should be 1). 3 periods reopened — investigate." |
| **Procurement Cycle Time** | Requisition to RFQ to Evaluation to PO to GRN to Payment | "Avg requisition-to-delivery: 28 days. Breakdown: Approval 3d, RFQ 8d, Vendor response 5d, Evaluation 4d, Delivery 8d. Biggest lever: RFQ stage — consider pre-qualified vendor lists." |
| **Reconciliation Health** | Bank reconciliation status, unmatched items, completion dates | "2 of 5 bank accounts unreconciled for 45+ days. Unmatched items: N890K. Auto-match rate: 62% — likely payee naming inconsistency." |
| **Workflow Automation ROI** | Rule execution counts, success/failure rates | "12 active rules processed 1,847 events. 94% success. 'Overdue invoice reminder' saved ~40 manual emails. But 'auto-approve small expenses' fails 23% — fix condition logic." |

**Coaching Actions**:
- "Delegate expense approvals above N100K to Smith's deputy while backlog clears"
- "Close process would be 4 days faster with daily AR reconciliation instead of month-end batch"
- "3 workflow rules have >10% failure rate — review trigger conditions"

### 3.3 Workforce Health

**Data Sources**: Performance, Attendance, Leave, Training, Discipline, Payroll, Skills, Expense, Tasks

| **Hiring Health** | Applicant funnel + time-to-fill | "Avg time-to-fill: 47 days (was 32). Bottleneck: Interview-to-Offer takes 18 days. 3 offers declined citing salary." |

| Analysis | Data Used | Insight Example |
|---|---|---|

### Analyzer Pattern

Each domain analyzer follows this pattern:

```python
class CashFlowAnalyzer:
    """Analyzes cross-module data for cash flow intelligence."""

    def __init__(self, db: Session) -> None:
        self.db = db

    def collect_data(self, org_id: UUID) -> dict:
        """Gather all cash-flow-relevant data (no raw PII)."""
        # Reads from AR, AP, Banking, Payroll, Expense services
        return {
            "ar_aging": {...},
            "ap_aging": {...},
            "bank_balances": [...],
            "upcoming_payroll": ...,
            "open_expenses": ...,
            "sales_pipeline": ...,
            "period": "2026-02",
        }

    def pre_analyze(self, data: dict) -> dict:
        """Compute metrics BEFORE LLM (cheaper, deterministic)."""
        return {
            "dso": calculate_dso(data["ar_aging"]),
            "dpo": calculate_dpo(data["ap_aging"]),
            "cash_conversion_cycle": ...,
            "30_day_forecast": ...,
            "top_overdue_customers": [...],
            "burn_rate": ...,
        }

    def generate_insights(self, org_id: UUID) -> list[CoachInsight]:
        """Full pipeline: collect -> pre-analyze -> LLM -> insights."""
        data = self.collect_data(org_id)
        metrics = self.pre_analyze(data)
        llm_response = self.insight_engine.analyze(
            prompt_template="cash_flow_analysis",
            context=metrics,
        )
        return self._parse_insights(llm_response, org_id)
```

**Key architectural principle**: Heavy computation (DSO, DPO, ratios, trends) is done deterministically in Python (free, instant, accurate). The LLM only handles **interpretation** and **coaching language** — what the numbers mean and what to do about them. This keeps token usage ~80% lower.

## 7. LLM Integration Strategy

### Provider Strategy (Hosted Llama + DeepSeek)

We will use hosted models behind an **OpenAI-compatible** API surface (Chat Completions), so the `InsightEngine` can remain provider-agnostic and we can swap hosts without rewriting business logic.

- **Llama (hosted)**: default for low-stakes, high-volume tasks (daily nudges, data-quality checks).
- **DeepSeek (hosted)**: default for higher-stakes reasoning and summarization (weekly/monthly reports, most interactive analysis).
- **Failover**: if the preferred backend is down or returns invalid structured output after repair retries, fall back to the other backend for the same tier.

### Token Budget & Cost Model

|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Note: hosted providers vary in pricing and in whether they return reliable `usage` (token counts). Budget enforcement must not depend solely on provider-reported usage.

### Cost Control Strategies

| Strategy | Implementation |
|---|---|
| Response caching | Redis with TTL (insights valid 24h, drafts not cached) |
| Batch processing | Celery tasks pre-generate insights overnight |
| Token budgeting | Per-org monthly limit in organization settings (use provider usage if present; otherwise estimate) |
| Tiered models | Simple tasks -> hosted Llama; Complex/strategic -> hosted DeepSeek |
| Structured output | Always request machine-validated JSON (schema-validated) |
| Pre-computation | Python does math; LLM does interpretation only |

### Structured Output Contract (Required)

All LLM calls must return **schema-valid JSON**.

- Define a Pydantic model per prompt type (e.g. cash flow analysis, insight list, report section).
- At runtime: `InsightEngine` validates the response JSON against the schema.
- If invalid: attempt up to `COACH_LLM_MAX_RETRIES` schema repair retries (same backend first, then failover backend).
- If still invalid: store a deterministic error record (and do not persist partial/ambiguous insights).

### Security, RBAC, and Tenant Isolation

- **Server-side only**: the browser never calls the LLM provider directly. No provider keys/tokens are exposed to `static/js/coach.js`.
- **Tenant scoping**: every analysis call is scoped by `organization_id` at the service layer; cache keys must include `organization_id`, `audience`, and `target_employee_id` (if any).
- **RBAC shaping**: prompt context must be assembled from role-filtered data. Example: customer/supplier names may only be included for finance-authorized principals; otherwise use codes.
- **Prompt injection stance** (chat): user messages are treated as untrusted. The engine must not expand data access based on user instructions; it can only use pre-approved analyzers and pre-built, role-filtered context bundles.

### Privacy & Anonymization

| Data Type | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Employee names | Replaced with codes (EMP-001) in LLM prompts; re-mapped in response |
| Customer/Supplier names | Replaced with codes; only shown if insight is for a finance user |
| Salary amounts | Aggregated (averages, ranges) never individual |
| Discipline details | Anonymized case codes only |
| Financial totals | Sent as-is (org-level, not personal) |

Implementation notes:
- Code-to-PII mapping should be ephemeral per request/task execution (in-memory) and must not be persisted to the DB or logs.
- Application logs must redact prompts/responses by default; store only minimal metadata needed for debugging and budget enforcement.

## Appendix B: RBAC Role-to-Insight Routing

The coaching engine uses RBAC roles to determine which insights to generate, deliver, and display for each user. This ensures users only see coaching relevant to their responsibilities.

### Permission-Based Insight Filtering

```
User logs in
    |
    v
WebAuthContext loaded (accessible_modules, roles, permissions)
    |
    v
CoachService.get_insights_for_user(user_id, org_id)
    |
    +-- Check user roles -> map to InsightAudience values
    +-- Check module access gates -> filter by category
    +-- Check target_employee_id -> personal vs org-wide
    |
    v
Return filtered insights sorted by severity + recency
```

### Tiered Insight Delivery

| Delivery Channel | Audience | Frequency | Content |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Dashboard cards** | All personas | Real-time (pre-computed) | Top 3 insights by severity for current role |
| **In-app notification** | All personas | Event-triggered | Urgent/warning severity insights only |
| **Weekly email digest** | Managers + Specialists | Monday 7 AM | Role-specific weekly coaching report |
| **Monthly report** | Directors + Executives | 1st of month | Cross-module strategic analysis |
| **Chat panel** | All personas (opt-in) | On demand | Natural language Q&A scoped to user's permissions |

### Data Access Scoping

The coaching engine inherits the user's data scope:

| Scope Type | Example | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| **Org-wide** | CFO sees all departments | `audience=EXECUTIVE` or `audience=HR` |
| **Department** | Manager sees own team only | Filter by `employee.reports_to_id` chain |
| **Self only** | Employee sees own data | Filter by `target_employee_id = current_user` |
| **Module-specific** | AR clerk sees AR data only | Filter by `category` matching accessible modules |
| **Approval-scoped** | Expense approver sees pending claims | Filter by approval delegation chain |

### External / Public Personas (No Coaching)

These personas exist in the system but are **excluded** from the coaching engine:

| Persona | Entry Point | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| External Job Candidate | `/careers/` | Public portal, no org data access |
| Onboarding Portal User | `/onboarding/start/{token}` | Token-based, temporary access |
